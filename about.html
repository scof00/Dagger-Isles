CV

How I Got Here

In 2022, I earned a Bachelor's Degree in Computer Engineering from West Virginia University. Throughout my final two years of undergraduate studies, I undertook internships at a nearby NASA facility, focusing on research and development in Static Code Analysis (SCA). Balancing part-time and full-time commitments, I pursued this opportunity until completing my degree. Following graduation, I seamlessly transitioned into a Master's Program in Electrical and Computer Engineering at Purdue University. In August 2023, I successfully graduated from Purdue and immediately embarked on a full-time role at Aon, where I currently serve as a Computer Science Litigation Consultant.

My Values & Beliefs

Hardwork

At the outset of my engineering studies, I encountered challenges with my mathematics courses. It was not a subject that came easily to me. To overcome this deficit, I began attending tutoring sessions, making use of office hours, and dedicating every evening to reviewing class materials. It was demanding, but rewarding as I figured out how to approach math in a way that I could understand. Now, whenever I encounter unfamiliar territory, I simply invest the necessary time and effort until I grasp the subject matter.

Practice Empathy

Accountability

I have found that the hardest person to hold accountable is yourself. Sometimes it's a little uncomfortable, it's certainly not very fun, but holding yourself accountable allows you to set reasonable expectations for others and encourages self-reflection before decision making.
