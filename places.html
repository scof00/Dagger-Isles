Ebonveil: Owned by the high court of elves, Ebonveil used to merely be an island home to an inactive volcano. Once it was discovered that veins of mithril were buried deep within its volcanic structure, a thriving metropolis quickly sprung up around it, turning it into one of the largest and busiest trading ports in all the seas.

    • Governor Jo-nah-tan Konz: Governor Jo-nah-tan was a former soldier in the army of the high elven court. After his time in the army, he was appointed as governor of Ebonveil, a position his grandfather used to hold. Although no one has any major gripe with Governor Konz’ leadership, it is well known he has a strong disdain for Ebonveil, and as such, he takes a very hands off approach.
    • The Veil Watch: Adorned in dark mithril armor, The Veil Watch are an unmistakable and imposing security force around town. Tasked with the safety of Ebonveil, The Veil Watch stands ever vigilant against all that would impose danger on the city, its constituents, or its economic importance to the High Elven Court.
    • Commander Seriyle Lanther: The head of The Veil Watch, Commander Seriyle is a former officer in the high elven army before being reassigned to Ebonveil.
    • Admiral Ella Aldaviel: Admiral in the high elven army, Ella Aldaviel is tasked with the safety of the Sea of Swords and warding off pirate attacks to Ebonveil, the surrounding islands, and trade vessels.
    • Brick and Brawn Mining Cooperate: After finding mithril veins buried beneath the island, the high elven court contracted all mining operations out to the Brick and Brawn Mining Cooperate, under very strict terms of course.
    • Duli Brickbrow: Current president of the Brick and Brawn Mining Cooperate. After being able to win the contract in Ebonveil, Duli took over the operation from his father and brought the company to new heights.
    • Wrossa’s Arcane Mechanics (WAM): Owned and run by the famed master artificer Finna Wrossa, WAM is the best blacksmith, ship maker, and craftsman. WAM offers a variety of services, many of which can be tailored to the customers needs. Besides the world renown master craftsman and his army of expert apprentices, WAM is also one of the few shipyards offering full dry dock services and arcane enhancements for vessels and people.
    • Finna Wrossa: Finna Wrossa is a gnome master craftsman artificer. Wrossa was originally brought to the island as a contractor under the dwarven mining operation in charge of mithril mining, but soon began to hone his abilities as a master craftsman and offer his services to the people of the Dagger Isles. Although no longer in mithril production, Wrossa still holds high importance in the running of Ebonveil, especially with the young Governor giving him room to work. Much of the infrastructure in Ebonveil is thanks to his genius.
    • Mage Magoo’s Magic Mansion: Mage Magoo was an old, retired adventurer who decided to settle on Ebonveil and open up his own magic emporium, primarily offering fun magical knick knacks to kids, and maybe some stronger magics to those offering the right price.

    • Raven’s Roost: The central hub for all pirate activity in the area, and more importantly, a safe refuge for pirates to call home while on land.

    • Bethany’s Bar, Brothel, Bank, and Brawl: Bethany’s B, B, B & B is every pirate's one stop shop for almost anything they could need while in Raven’s Roost. As the largest building on the island, this building is hobbled together from ships that Dread Pirate Bethany had conquered and dismantled on her journeys. Here, pirates of all calibers can participate in a variety of vices, while also providing rest, safety and comfort.
    • Dread Pirate Bethany Coborel: Bethany Coborel, a 200 year old drow, is one of the most feared pirates to ever grace the Sea of Swords. Although retired, Captain Coborel wields the fear and respect earned through her long and storied career as a pirate to keep the peace and security in Raven’s Roost. In addition to owning the B, B, B & B, Bethany is also the de-facto governing body of Raven’s Roost, ensuring all pirates abide by the rules and don’t cause too much trouble.
    • Raven’s Roost Boatyard: The primary repair yard in Raven’s Roost. Ran by Rayndas Rowntree, a 35 year old human, this shipyard offers the best repairs in Raven’s Roost, and offers a moderate selection of upgrades for your vessel. After all, she is the disgraced apprentice of Finnna Wrossa. While she does run the premier place for ship repairs, there are other places in Raven’s Roost, although the quality is never ensured.
    • Shazir’s Apothecary and Alchemical Libations: The elder tiefling wizard known as Shazir, upon settling in Raven’s Roost, opened up his shop to sell potions and magical components to those who wander through his store, but he only has 2 main passions in life: The blackmarket components and tomes pirates find on their adventures, and his love for gambling.
    • The Rockdorf Weaponsmith: Oogorim Rockdorf is a sturdy half-orc blacksmith. His love for the ocean and longing to be a pirate was thwarted by one thing: his crippling ocean sickness. Instead, this self taught blacksmith dedicates his days to making crude weapons for both the ship and her crews, blessing them all in the way of Tempus.
    • Raven’s Tabernacle: A modest building of worship run by Sadon deGrey, an elvish cleric. While this place of worship is run by a cleric of Umberlee, worship of all gods is permitted and welcome here.

    • Other Locations:

    • The Whisper Cay Chain: A chain of small fishing islands consisting of the islands of: Whisper Shoal, Whisper Glade, and the Whispering Mangrove.
    • Sapphire Isle: A get-away location for rich nobles and merchants.
    • Golden Sands Island: A common docking point for merchants midway between the Sword Coast and the Dagger Isles.
    • Frostbite Atoll: The northernmost island in the Sea of Swords, mostly left uninhabited.
    • Misty Bay: Mainly a residential island.
    • Gladia: One of the larger islands of the Dagger Isles that is used primarily for agricultural production, storage, and logistics for the rest of the islands.
    • There are other small islands dotted throughout the Sea of Swords that are either unclaimed, uninhabited, or uncharted. Who knows what lies among them?
